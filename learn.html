Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about blockchain investing

What is blockchain technology?
Blockchain is a distributed, decentralized, public ledger technology that records transactions across many computers so that any involved record cannot be altered retroactively, without the alteration of all subsequent blocks. This allows the participants to verify and audit transactions independently and relatively inexpensively.
How do I evaluate a blockchain project before investing?
When evaluating a blockchain project, consider factors such as: the team's experience and track record, the problem the project aims to solve, the technology and innovation behind it, tokenomics and distribution model, market potential, roadmap and milestones, community engagement, and competition in the space. It's also important to review their whitepaper, audit reports, and connect with team members if possible.
What are the risks associated with blockchain investments?
Blockchain investments carry several risks including: market volatility, regulatory uncertainty, technology risks, security vulnerabilities, project failure, liquidity issues, and potential scams or fraudulent projects. It's important to conduct thorough research, diversify your investments, and only invest what you can afford to lose.
How can I get started with blockchain investing?
To get started with blockchain investing: 1) Educate yourself about blockchain technology and different investment options, 2) Set up secure accounts on reputable exchanges or platforms, 3) Start with a small amount and consider established projects first, 4) Gradually diversify your portfolio as you gain experience, 5) Keep up with industry news and trends, and 6) Consider different investment strategies such as dollar-cost averaging.
What is the difference between tokens and coins?
Coins (like Bitcoin or Ethereum) have their own blockchain and primarily function as currency or a store of value. Tokens are built on existing blockchains (like many ERC-20 tokens on Ethereum) and can represent various assets or utilities within a specific project's ecosystem. While coins primarily serve as money, tokens can represent assets, access rights, ownership, or specific functionalities within a network.
